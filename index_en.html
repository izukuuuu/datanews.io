Chapter 2

International Students in China and Cultural Adaptation

Cultural adaptation refers to the process by which individuals, after entering a new cultural environment, gradually adjust to the new cultural norms and values through learning and adaptation. This process aims to reduce cultural conflicts and stress, ultimately improving satisfaction and efficiency in daily life and work. Cultural adaptation is a complex and dynamic process influenced by various factors related to individuals and their environment. International students in China, as cross-cultural individuals, experience a complex and dynamic cultural adaptation process influenced by multiple factors related to individuals and their environment.

Students from all corners of the world, carrying their aspirations for the future, have set foot on this mysterious land of China.

With curiosity in their hearts, they explore this nation with its long history and vibrant culture, becoming a crucial driving force for international cultural exchange.

Our data collection primarily employs two methods: surveys and in-depth interviews.
Surveys come in both online and offline forms,
with bilingual surveys designed on the WeChat Questionnaire Star platform for online use and interviews conducted in English, Chinese, and Spanish for offline data collection.
We primarily selected a group of international students from Beijing Jiaotong University,
utilizing systematic sampling based on nationality, gender, and other factors,
with plans to extend the study to various universities in Beijing in the future.
In-depth interviews are conducted with select survey participants to gather more information and personal stories.

According to the survey,
approximately 60% of respondents reported difficulties in adapting to China's education and learning methods,
including unfamiliarity with different teaching styles, assessment methods, and academic culture.

Over 70% of respondents mentioned that learning and using Chinese language posed a major challenge during their study in China,
indicating that language barriers may be a significant cultural adaptation issue for international students.

About 45% of respondents reported challenges in dealing with daily life matters in China,
including accommodation arrangements, public transportation, shopping, and adapting to the use of Chinese currency.

Nearly 30% of respondents noted difficulties in forming friendships with Chinese students,
which can be attributed to cultural differences, language barriers, or differences in social environments.

Approximately 20% of respondents mentioned challenges in maintaining physical health,
including adapting to different climate, diet, and lifestyle.

About 50% of respondents reported experiencing academic pressure during their study in China,
which may be related to differences in the learning environment and requirements.

Language barriers are one of the most noticeable challenges.
According to in-depth interview findings, many international students in China expressed that language barriers are their most significant challenge:

"It took me two years to understand Mandarin."
"I often forget how to write Chinese characters."
"I don't know how to express my feelings in Chinese."

In addition to language barriers, international students in China also face cultural differences between East and West.
These cultural differences often lead to emotional responses such as cultural conflict, cultural disorientation, or cultural alienation among the international students.

In this study, we also found that most international students in China prefer to use foreign social platforms such as WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok.
In contrast, their engagement on China's mainstream social platforms is relatively low, with only about 10% of respondents indicating the use of platforms like Douyin (TikTok), Weibo, Bilibili, and Xiaohongshu.
We further explored the reasons behind this phenomenon, and many respondents stated that they prefer foreign social platforms due to established habits and the significant differences in functionality and usage patterns compared to platforms they are familiar with.

"I tried, but I found their functions and usage to be unfamiliar, so I prefer using those platforms."
"Using foreign social platforms gives me a sense of belonging because I can stay in touch with my original friends. On China's social platforms, I might feel a bit lonely."
……

Regarding why they do not use Chinese platforms like Xiaohongshu or Weibo, many respondents also cited usage difficulties.
One respondent mentioned:

"The translation function is not very useful."

Another international student stated:

"In many places, like comments, I can't translate them, which creates difficulties in my usage."

These responses highlight the language barriers that may hinder the use of Chinese social media platforms for international students.
Language difficulties while using these platforms can affect their interaction with Chinese internet users and create inconvenience in information access.

Chapter 3

International Students in China and Cultural Communication

In today's era of globalization, education transcends national borders, and international students serve as bridges connecting different cultures and countries. International students in China not only promote their own cultures but also spread Chinese culture to the world.

Through various activities such as foreign cultural festivals and cultural weeks, international students bring diverse foreign cultures to the campus, merging them with Chinese culture. Their words and songs, like colorful wings, gracefully traverse the language barriers, creating beautiful harmonies with Chinese culture. International students bring the richness of diverse foreign cultures to the campus, forming a captivating scene of cultural diversity. "World Culture Festival," "World Culture Week"... In these vibrant foreign cultural festivals, international students become messengers of cultural exchange, competing on stage to showcase the splendid cultures of different nations.

Here, they are the meeting point of Chinese and foreign cultures, a place where minds from across borders connect. International students in China are not only conveyors of exotic cultures but also serve as organic channels for spreading Chinese culture abroad. While studying, witnessing, and sharing Chinese politics, economics, and culture, they provide the world with a window to understand the real China. They play a crucial role as bridges in Sino-foreign exchanges, showcasing the true face of our country to the world. In the beautiful campus, international students from all over the world gather, learn, communicate, and grow together. They infuse new vitality into Chinese culture and spread our outstanding traditional culture to every corner of the world.
In our survey, Mary, an international student from France, was deeply captivated by Chinese calligraphy during her studies in China. She not only diligently practiced it herself but also actively introduced the art of calligraphy to her friends in France. After returning to France, Mary, along with like-minded friends, established a calligraphy association and regularly organized calligraphy exhibitions and exchange events, allowing more people to appreciate the charm of Chinese traditional culture. Furthermore, many international students share Chinese traditional festivals on social media with friends from around the world. For example, during the Spring Festival, international students showcase the unique atmosphere of the Chinese New Year through photos and videos, featuring traditions like pasting couplets, making dumplings, and setting off firecrackers. These customs, rich in Chinese characteristics, attract the attention of people from all over the world, allowing more people to understand Chinese traditional culture and customs. They provide a window to the world to understand the real China through their actions, playing a crucial role in presenting our country's true face to the world.

Chapter 4

Outlook

As the global pandemic gradually comes under control, the lives of international students in China will return to normal. During the pandemic, many international students had to engage in online learning and remote communication with their teachers and peers in their home countries. While this mode of communication allowed them to continue their studies, it couldn't replace face-to-face interaction and engagement. In the post-pandemic era, we hope that international students in China will actively participate in cross-cultural communication. In their studies and daily lives, they can proactively engage with people from different countries and regions, understanding diverse cultures and ways of thinking. This will not only broaden their horizons but also enhance their cross-cultural communication skills. Additionally, we hope that international students can use their pandemic experiences to explore new ways of cross-cultural communication. For example, they can organize cross-cultural exchange activities through online communication platforms, involving more people. They can also use social media tools to build cross-cultural communities, promoting interaction and communication between different cultures. In the field of cross-cultural communication, we also need to provide psychological support and counseling to international students. Many of them face various pressures and challenges, such as loneliness, anxiety, and depression. Therefore, it's necessary to offer appropriate psychological support and counseling to help them better adapt to their new environment, alleviate stress, and manage negative emotions.

International students in China are not only ambassadors of cultural exchange but also promoters of Chinese culture. We hope that through cultural interactions, they can better spread Chinese culture to a global audience, allowing more people to understand and appreciate China. By organizing relevant activities, international students can deeply study and experience Chinese culture. Through reading Chinese classic literature, participating in various cultural events, learning traditional Chinese arts and crafts, they can gain a profound understanding and experience of Chinese culture. They can transmit the authentic and profound aspects of Chinese culture to the world. Furthermore, we hope that international students can use their own language and methods to propagate Chinese culture to the world. By using social media platforms, they can share their experiences of life and study in China, allowing more people to learn about China. At the same time, through their actions, they can showcase the values and spirit of Chinese culture, enabling more people to feel the charm of Chinese culture. Chinese culture is extensive and profound. Only with an open and inclusive attitude towards cultural differences and conflicts can effective dissemination of Chinese culture occur. By respecting and understanding the existence and development of different cultures, with an attitude of equality and mutual trust, different cultures can interact and collide. In this way, international students in China can maximize their role in spreading Chinese culture and truly become ambassadors and bridges of cultural exchange, promoting communication and understanding among different cultures.
