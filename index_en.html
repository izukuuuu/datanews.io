    前言

    在华留学生：
    文化的“跨界”使者

    我国一直以来都欢迎世界各地的留学生来华学习，我国对留学生提供了一系列的优惠政策，包括奖学金、简化签证手续、扩大就业范围等。如教育部《关于规范我高等学校接受国际学生有关工作的通知》等，均为国际学生进入中国高等院校本专科阶段学习提供了良好的政策。随着我国国际地位的不断提高，来华留学生的数量也在逐年增加。在华留学生在享受我国提供的便利的同时，也需要适应我国的文化和生活方式，这是一个双向的过程，也是世界了解我国的一个重要途径。

    截至2022年底，我国共有来自214个国家和地区的25.5万名在华留学生。

    数据来源：《中国教育统计年鉴》（2012-2022）

    其中亚洲国家占比最高，达到66.9%。

    其次为非洲，占比为22.5%，
    成为既亚洲之外的第二大来华留学输出地。

    数据来源：《中国教育统计年鉴》（2022）

    对于在华留学生而言，跨越国界仅是开始，更为重要的是他们在文化领域的跨界融合。

    不仅仅是因为身处中国而跨越国界，更因为他们在文化层面展开了深刻的“跨界”探索。

    如今，在华留学生已逐渐崭露头角，成为国际文化交流的关键推动力。

    他们在中国学习、生活、工作，不仅深刻感受中国文化的魅力，同时也将自己的文化特色传播开来。

    这种文化交融与传播，正是“跨界”的真正本质，使留学生成为文化的“跨界”使者。

    第二章

    在华留学生与文化适应

    来自五湖四海的留学生们，带着对未来的憧憬，踏上了中国这片神秘的土地。

    他们带着好奇心，探索着这个拥有悠久历史、灿烂文化的国度。成为国际文化交流的关键推动力。

    来自五湖四海的留学生们，
    带着对未来的憧憬，
    踏上了中国这片神秘的土地。

    他们带着好奇心，
    探索着这个拥有悠久历史、灿烂文化的国度。
    成为国际文化交流的关键推动力。

    文化适应指个体在进入一个新的文化环境后，通过学习和调整，逐渐适应新的文化规范和价值观，从而减少文化冲突和压力，提高生活和工作的满意度和效率的过程。文化适应是一个复杂而动态的过程，受到个体和环境的多种因素的影响。在华留学生作为跨文化者，他们在中国所经历的文化适应过程也因个体与环境的多重因素而变得复杂而动态。

    我们对北京高校的在华留学生展开了问卷与访谈调查，问题主要集中于留学生的跨文化适应。根据相关结果，我们收集到了关于在中国学习期间遇到的主要困难的相关数据。

    根据调查，约有 60% 的受访者表示在中国学习期间，他们遇到了适应中国的教育和学习方法方面的困难，
    包括对不同的教学风格、考核方式和学术文化的不熟悉。

    超过 70% 的受访者提到，学习和使用汉语是他们在中国学习过程中面临的主要挑战之一，
    这表明语言障碍对留学生来说可能是一个重要的文化适应问题。

    大约 45% 的受访者表示在中国处理日常生活问题时遇到了挑战，
    包括住宿安排、搭乘公共交通、购物以及适应中国货币的使用等。

    近 30% 的受访者提到他们在与中国学生建立友谊关系方面遇到了困难，
    涉及到文化差异、语言障碍或社交环境的不同。

    大约 20% 的受访者表示在保持身体健康方面存在挑战，
    包括适应不同的气候、饮食和生活方式等。

    约有 50% 的受访者提到他们在中国学习期间面临学业压力，可能与不同的学习环境和要求有关。

    具体而言，语言障碍可能是最直观的困难之一。
    根据调查，有许多在华留学生表示，语言障碍是最大的挑战：

    “对我来说听力是最难的，当我对别人说对不起，我没听清时，他们只是重复一样的话，说的更慢一些。但我需要的是他们换一个近义词来替换我不理解的地方。”

    “一些中文词汇的词义对于外国人来说很难理解，有的时候我很想和一个人交流，但是当我不能理解时谈话就难以再继续下去了。”

    “我很难听懂很多人的口音，就算是细微的口音差异都会让我难以理解，一些很普通的词在稍微不同的读音下对我而言很难听懂。”

    除了语言障碍外，在华留学生还要面对中西方文化之间的差异。
    这些文化差异在无形之间也导致在华留学生产生文化冲突、文化失落或者文化隔阂的情绪。

    在本次研究中，我们还发现大多数在华留学生倾向于使用国外的社交平台，
    如 WhatsApp、Instagram、Facebook 和 Tiktok 等。
    就如日常的通讯社交而言，很多在华留学生一开始根本没有听说过微信，也不知道如何注册、使用。

    “有一次我在网上和一个女生交流，她提出我们可以加一些绿色的东西，我上网搜索了很久也不知道她是什么意思，后来我明白她是在指微信。”

    “当你刚认识一个中国人时，人们很容易习惯性地要加你的微信。这对我来说也有一些不适应，在一些情况下，对于那些之前没有接触过的人，我可能会拒绝加微信好友，或者加了也没有聊过。”

    于此同时，在华留学生在我国的主流社交平台上活跃度较低，
    在我们的调查中，仅约 10%的受访者表示使用抖音，微博、B 站、小红书等平台的使用率则更低。

    针对这一现象，我们进一步探讨了原因。
    许多受访者表示，他们之所以倾向于使用国外社交平台，
    一方面是因为已经形成了使用习惯，
    另一方面则是因为我国的社交平台在功能和使用习惯上与他们所熟悉的平台存在较大差异。

    “试过，但是我发现我对它们的功能和使用方式不太熟悉，所以我还是更喜欢使用那些平台。”
    “我觉得使用国外的社交平台让我更有归属感，因为我可以与我原来的朋友们保持联系。而在我国的社交平台上，我可能会感到有些孤独。”
    ……

    而对于为何不使用国内的小红书、微博等平台，不少受访者还提出了使用方面的困难，
    其中一位受访者表示：

    “翻译功能不是很好用。”

    另一位留学生则提到：

    “很多地方，像是评论没法翻译，这对我的使用造成了困难”。

    由此可见，除了线下学习与生活中遇到的语言上的障碍，当前网络环境中，受相关翻译功能的限制以及影响，留学生也不能很好的参与线上社交互动，以及信息的获取。

    Overseas Students in China and Cultural Communication

    In today's era of globalization, education transcends national borders, and overseas students become bridges connecting different cultures and countries. Overseas students in China not only spread their own cultures but also sow Chinese culture to the world.

    By organizing various activities such as foreign cultural festivals, cultural weeks, etc., overseas students bring rich and colorful exotic cultures into the campus, blending with Chinese culture. Their words and songs, like colorful wings, lightly traverse the barriers of language and weave into a beautiful melody with Chinese culture. Overseas students bring diverse and rich exotic cultures to the campus, forming a landscape of cultural diversity. "World Cultural Festival", "World Cultural Week"... In these colorful foreign cultural festivals, foreign students become messengers of cultural exchange, competing to showcase the splendid cultures of different nationalities on stage.

    Here, they are the intersection of Chinese and foreign cultures, the meeting point of minds across borders.
    Overseas students in China are not only disseminators of exotic cultures but also an organic channel for the dissemination of Chinese culture abroad.
    While studying, witnessing, and disseminating Chinese politics, economy, and culture, they provide a window for countries around the world to understand the real China.
    Playing an important role as a bridge in Sino-foreign exchanges, they have played an important role in showing the true face of China to the world.
    In the beautiful campus, students from all over the world gather together to learn, communicate, and grow together. They not only inject new vitality into Chinese culture but also spread excellent traditional Chinese culture to every corner of the world.

    She not only studied hard herself but also actively introduced calligraphy art to her friends in France.
    After returning to France, Mary and her like-minded friends founded a calligraphy association,
    regularly organizing calligraphy exhibitions and exchange activities, letting more people understand the charm of Chinese traditional culture.

    Moreover, many overseas students also share Chinese traditional festivals on social media with friends from all over the world.
    For example, during the Spring Festival, overseas students showcase the unique atmosphere of the Chinese New Year through photos and videos, such as pasting couplets, making dumplings, and setting off firecrackers.
    These customs with Chinese characteristics attract the attention of people from all over the world, allowing more people to understand Chinese traditional culture and customs.

    Chapter Four

    Outlook

    Since the 18th National Congress of the Communist Party of China, General Secretary Xi Jinping has given instructions and instructions on several occasions on the work of studying abroad in China and has written to overseas students in China several times, fully reflecting the caring concern of the Party Central Committee for the work of studying abroad in the new era and affirming and encouraging the front line of studying abroad in China.

    On May 17, 2020, General Secretary Xi Jinping replied to all Pakistani students at Beijing University of Science and Technology, mentioning the expectations for overseas students in China, hoping that overseas students would learn more about China and tell the world what they see in China.

    In June 2021, General Secretary Xi Jinping replied to the international students at Peking University, hoping that foreign students would learn more about China and tell the world what they see in China.

    On January 17, 2024, General Secretary Xi Jinping replied to Kenyan students and alumni representatives at Beijing Jiaotong University, encouraging them to continue to shine for the cause of Sino-Kenyan and Sino-African friendship. In the letter, General Secretary Xi Jinping mentioned that the friendship between China and Kenya has a long history, and the joint construction of the "Belt and Road" initiative has turned the ideal of revitalizing Sino-Kenyan development into reality, closely linking the well-being of the people of the two countries. General Secretary Xi Jinping emphasized that looking forward to the future, the magnificent picture of the "Belt and Road" and the grand blueprint of comprehensive strategic partnership between China and Kenya need more promising young people, including overseas students, to achieve. Overseas students have become witnesses, beneficiaries, builders, and disseminators of Sino-Kenyan and Sino-African friendly cooperation because of this "road to happiness" connecting them with China. He hopes that overseas students will learn their professional knowledge well, carry forward the traditional friendship, engage in cooperation between the two countries, tell good stories of Sino-African friendship, and make greater contributions to promoting the construction of a high-level Sino-African community with a shared future.

    With the gradual control of the global epidemic, the lives of overseas students in China will gradually return to normal. During the epidemic, many overseas students had to conduct online learning in their own countries and communicate remotely with their teachers and classmates. Although online communication can maintain the progress of learning, it cannot replace face-to-face communication and interaction.

    Against the background of the gradual control of the global epidemic, for overseas students in China, life is gradually returning to normal. In this process, we must recognize the challenges and difficulties that overseas students returning to offline and campus life have experienced.

    Considering this, it is necessary to first focus on the practical problems that overseas students face in their study and life in China. Issues such as identity authentication, opening bank accounts, and binding bank cards during their stay in China still hinder the living environment of overseas students in China. For overseas students who can now return to offline communication, this is an important turning point. But at the same time, corresponding difficulties have also been encountered. Jia Qingguo, a member of the CPPCC National Committee, proposed relevant suggestions at this year's two sessions, mentioning in the proposals "Proposal to Increase the Attraction of Foreign Students to Study in China" and "Proposal to Encourage Civilian Work to Tell Good Stories about China", Foreign students in China urgently need to solve difficulties such as identity authentication, opening bank accounts, and binding bank cards during their stay in China to optimize their learning and living environment.

    Furthermore, in the post-epidemic era, we hope that overseas students in China can actively participate in intercultural communication. At the same time, we can also explore new ways of intercultural communication based on the experience during the epidemic. Cross-cultural communication activities can be organized through online communication platforms to involve more people. Utilizing social media and other tools to establish cross-cultural communities to promote communication and interaction between different cultures.

    In the field of cross-cultural adaptation, we also need to strengthen psychological support and counseling for overseas students. Many overseas students face various pressures and challenges such as loneliness, anxiety, and depression. Therefore, appropriate psychological support and counseling need to be provided to help them better adapt to the new environment and alleviate stress and negative emotions.

    Overseas students in China are not only messengers of cultural exchange but also disseminators of Chinese culture. We hope to better spread Chinese culture through the collision of souls and through the group of overseas students in China, so that more people can understand and understand China. Through relevant activities, let overseas students deeply study and experience Chinese culture. By reading Chinese classic literature, participating in various cultural activities, learning Chinese traditional arts and skills, and other methods, deepen the understanding and experience of Chinese culture. Spread real and profound Chinese culture to the world. Secondly, we hope that overseas students can spread Chinese culture to the world in their own languages and ways. Using social media platforms to share their life and learning experiences in China, so that more people can understand China. At the same time, they can also demonstrate the values and spiritual outlook of Chinese culture through their actions, allowing more people to feel the charm of Chinese culture. Chinese culture is profound and extensive, and only by adopting an open and inclusive attitude towards cultural differences and conflicts can Chinese culture be effectively disseminated. Respect and understanding for the existence and development of different cultures, and collision of cultural exchanges with equality and mutual trust. In this way, we can maximize the role of overseas students in China in spreading Chinese culture and truly become messengers and bridges of cultural exchange, promoting communication and understanding between different cultures.
